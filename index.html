About

I fix the systems behind the support, not just the tickets in front of me.

I'm a Customer Support & CRM Specialist who works inside HubSpot to close the gaps that cost businesses money — slow response times, cold leads, and processes that rely on someone remembering to do something manually.

My background started in frontline support, which is where I learned that most "support problems" are actually process problems. A slow reply usually traces back to bad ticket routing. A lost invoice usually traces back to a workflow that depends on memory instead of automation. I build the systems so nothing depends on memory.

The deeper I got into automation, the more I noticed the part nobody talks about: every workflow you connect is also a door someone could walk through. So I picked up security-aware automation practices alongside the CRM work — access audits, API and webhook hygiene, spotting the phishing attempts aimed at support teams specifically. Not because I'm trying to be a security expert, but because "it works" and "it's safe" should be the same standard.

Featured work

Building an invoice-tracking workflow for a fuel distribution network.

Juliet Petroleum

Fuel distribution · B2B invoicing

The problem

Invoices between individual filling stations and corporate customers were regularly misplaced or simply never submitted to the customer's accounts team. That created payment delays and gaps in reporting between the stations and corporate accounts.

The solution

I built an automated workflow that logs every station sale as an invoice, automatically notifies the corporate customer's accountant when a new invoice is issued, and sends a confirmation update once payment is received — so no invoice depends on someone remembering to send it.

Cross-Platform Lead Sync

LinkedIn automation · CRM sync

The problem

Leads and customer signals were scattered across LinkedIn, the CRM, web forms, and e-commerce platforms, with no way to act on them without manually copying data between tools — outreach was slow and contacts were easy to lose track of.

The solution

I built a two-way Make.com automation around Dux-Soup. New LinkedIn activity automatically updates HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, Google Contacts/Sheets, and Facebook retargeting audiences, and notifies the team on Slack. In reverse, new leads from Google Forms, HubSpot, WordPress, and WooCommerce automatically trigger a LinkedIn connection request or direct message — closing the loop in both directions.
